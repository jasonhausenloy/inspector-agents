What this is

The Verifier Challenge — the project Jason Hausenloy and Jasmine Li are proposing — is the DARPA-Grand-Challenge equivalent for AI verification: an open, global competition to build the best system for inferring what's running inside a datacenter from its logs.

This MVP is the worked example. We trained a small but real GPT on a MacBook, captured the rawest logs we honestly can on Apple Silicon, and built a Claude agent that reads them. No deterministic rule engine, no cheat sheet — just structured prompting and the trace.

What this isn't

One chip. Single host. No DCGM, no NVLink, no per-GPU power, no multi-host gradient sync. The laptop demo is the proof of concept; closing the gap to frontier verification is what the Challenge funds. The honest accounting of what's missing →
